		\section*{Radioactive Decay}
		\section{Introduction}
		Consider a radioactive decay problem involving two types of nuclei, $A$ and $B$, with populations $N_A(t)$ and $N_B(t)$. Suppose that type $A$ nuclei decay to form type $B$ nuclei, which then also decay, according to the differential equations
		\begin{equation}
			\frac{dN_A}{dt}=-\frac{N_A}{\tau_A}
		\end{equation}
		\begin{equation}
			\frac{dN_B}{dt}=\frac{N_A}{\tau_A}-\frac{N_B}{\tau_B}
		\end{equation}
		Here, we assume that type A nuclei are naturally occurring, and type B are not. This paper focuses on determing the age of an object by measuring the amount of type A and B nuclei within the object. We then discuss the accuracy of the age based on the ratio of measured nuclei $\dfrac{N_B}{N_A}$
		\section{Methods}
		The original Euler method is used. The form
		\[f(t+\Delta t)=f(t)+\frac{df}{dt}(t)\Delta t\]
		\indent is adapted into our coupled differential equations. We obtain the iteration relations
		\begin{equation}
			N_A(t+\Delta t) = N_B(t) -\frac{N_A(t)}{\tau_A}\Delta t
		\end{equation}
		\begin{equation}
			N_B(t+\Delta t) = N_B(t)
		\end{equation}
		The following parameters were initialized as such:
		\begin{enumerate}
			\item $N_A(0) = 1$~~~{\textit{\footnotesize (Note: this represents 100\% of the starting nuclei being type A.)}}
			\item $N_B(0) = 0$
			\item $\Delta t=0.1$
			\item Type A Decay Constant $\tau_A$
			\item Type B Decay Constant $\tau_B$ 
			\item Scaled time $T = t/\tau_A$
		\end{enumerate}
		Different decay ratios $\gamma = \tau_A/\tau/B$ were tested.
